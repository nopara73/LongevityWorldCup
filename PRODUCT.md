# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Longevity athletes, prospective competitors, skeptical observers, media, and researchers who need to inspect a public biological-age competition. They arrive to understand the standings, verify proof, compare athletes, join a track, or audit the rules behind a score.

## Product Purpose

Longevity World Cup is a public age-reversal sport. It ranks athletes by verified biological-age results, exposes proof and event history, and gives new competitors a path to submit eligible biomarkers. Success means a cold visitor quickly understands that this is a real competition with athletes, rankings, proof, rules, and stakes.

## Brand Personality

Serious, official, competitive. The product should feel like a global sport with scoreboards and records, not a wellness blog, SaaS dashboard, crypto campaign, or generic startup landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic card-heavy SaaS templates, soft wellness editorial pages, fake-premium gradients, crypto-dashboard styling, playful game UI, AI-generated hype copy, and vague motivational claims. Do not hide the actual leaderboard, athlete faces, proof, or competition mechanics behind abstract posters or decorative effects.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the competition: show real standings, athletes, rank, score, and proof before decoration.
2. Make proof inspectable: scores need nearby rules, evidence, dates, events, and public-record cues.
3. Keep the tone official: use restrained sport language, clear actions, and concrete labels.
4. Preserve product reality: the leaderboard is the product, and every supporting page should route back to it.
5. Polish through hierarchy: improve clarity, density, spacing, and trust before adding visual novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible, keyboard-usable public pages with visible focus states, readable contrast, named controls, reduced-motion respect, and responsive layouts that remain understandable on small phones, tablets, and desktop screens.
